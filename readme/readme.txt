*5: things often found in the game
there is a couple starters that must be clarified. If you don't understand some of them now, you will after reading more of the readme. These are listed below.
dlg's: These are basically short for in game dialog. It's just like something popping up with an OK button, just like a simple message. Accept for in here, your ok button is enter, and you can repeat the text in the dlg by pressing any of the 4 arrow keys.
skippable track: Pretty self explanatory, these are simply audio files that play and can be skipped. When it is playing, just press enter and it fades out the currently playing track that is meant to be skippable.
virtual input box: These are boxes that you type input. However they are not normal input boxes. the f2 key toggles character repeat. You can type like normal, and enter submits. Escape cancels.
aliases. When you start the game, a file called aliases.txt is created. In this file, you can make chat replacements for things you may type often. An example would you like this.
mhd=mahdi
This would replace any instance of mhd in your chat with mahdi. Say you typed /pm mhd hi. It would replace it with /pm mahdi hi, effectively PMIng mahdi.
script keys: these make it quick to send chats or type commands you use often. They can be defined per line in scriptkeys.txt and can be accessed by typing numbers when online

*8: commands
To chat with players, press Slash. This will bring up a text box where you can type your message. Once you finish writing your message, press enter to send the message for the whole entire server to see!
In the chat box, press left and right arrows to jump by character, and use the up and down arrows to review the message. Press Home and End to jump to the start or end of the message, and Backspace to delete the previous character.
You will hear what you are typing each time you input a character. You can press f1 to toggle this between off or on.
You will hear a beep when you enter a capital letter. You can press F2 to toggle this between beep, speak cap, or no difference.
The chat box also supports slash commands, in which you type /command and then parameters. A few of these are below.

*10: inventory
**10.1: In the inventory
Once you are in the inventory, you can use the up and down arrows to scroll threw items. You will hear item. You have x. Ware item is the item you have such as knife, fire_starter, etc and x is the number of that items you have such as 9nn_ammo. You have 35. Once you selected an item you want to use, you need to press either left arrow, or right arrow to get that item in to either your left or right hand. Or, to drop an item in your inventory, press the delete key on that item. If you press enter on it, you will get a description of the item and you can therefor get more details about that item sometimes. Pressing escape, exits the inventory. 
**10.2. Outside of the inventory
Once you have done what you need to in the inventory, you press escape to exit it. Then, if you had gotten an item you want to use, press the 1 key for your left hand and the 2 key for your right hand. For example if you put a knife in your left hand and a branch in your right hand, you would press 1 to access the knife and 2 to access the branch. Once you have the item you want to use, press the space bar to use that item. Note that this item must be in your hand, by pressing either 1 or 2, similar to weapons in most fps stile games. Double tap 1 or too to put that item back in your bag and return that hand to being empty. This is usually how you pick things up, with an empty hand. Another thing you are going to want to know how to do, is to interact with items. 
**10.3: Interacting.
To interact, press t. What this does is makes one hand, interact with another hand. For example, too empty hands, make you clap. A wood in one hand and an wood in the other, will make long_wood. Etc. Just put the first item in your left hand, the second in your right hand, and you will be good to go!

**19.10: pong
Here is how it works.
You are on a 2d grid, you are on one end and your opponent is on the other end.
Your goal is to hit the ball back and forth to each other with a few twists.
The ball can move left and right
hit left and right arrows to move left and right. you will hear step sounds to indicate this. if you are at the left or right edges, you hear another sound.
To serve or receive the ball, hit up arrow.
when the ball is hit back to you, you hear so by a whack sound and the ball begins to get louder. move closer with left and right, and hit up arrow to receive the ball.
the ball goes back for the oponent to receive and so on.

**19.17: swimming

**19.18: destroy the door
Basically, go through the level, left and right, killing the doors. Each time a door is killed, you have another one spawning somewhere else. Find and kill that one. Once they are all dead, which is after 20 doors, then you win. Hit D to check your number and how many doors are left

**21.1: Coordinate explanation
minx: Means minimum x.
maxx: Means maximum x.
miny: Means minimum y.
maxy: Means maximum y.
minz: Means minimum z.
Maxz: Means maximum z.
X is left and right movement.
Y is forward and backward movement.
Z is up and down movement.
**21.2: map making
/newmap mapname, max x, max y, max z, platform
map name is name of you're map, such as test
max x is maximom right movement. Such as 500
max y is maximom forward movement. Such as 200
max z is maximom up movement, such as 100
platform is the serfice that you'd like to walk on it such as grass, gravel, concrete
for example:
/newmap test 100 100 100 grass
then after pressing enter, you'll moved to you're new map
